Triangle Center Loci

Introduction — Thales Circle

Triangle Centers on the Thales Configuration

This explorer follows a moving triangle in the Thales configuration: $A = (1,0)$ and $B = (-1,0)$ stay fixed while $C = (\cos\theta, \sin\theta)$ slides around the unit circle $x^2 + y^2 = 1$. Each choice of $\theta$ creates a new triangle, and the chosen ETC center $X_n$ traces a locus.

What you are seeing
Think of this page as a synchronized geometry lab: the orange point moves, the triangle updates, and a classical center such as the incenter, centroid, or symmedian point responds instantly.
  • The blue segment $AB$ is fixed and acts like a stable ruler.
  • The orange point $C$ is the "input" of the experiment.
  • The gold trace is the path swept out by the selected center $X_n$.
Why Thales makes life cleaner
Because $AB$ is a diameter of the circumcircle, Thales' theorem gives a right angle at $C$. So every non-degenerate triangle in this widget has $\angle C = 90°$.
$$a = |BC| = 2\left|\cos\tfrac{\theta}{2}\right|$$
Those side lengths are exactly the data used in the barycentric formulas that define many triangle centers.
From classical geometry to coordinates
The README's main idea is that the moving centers can be rewritten as rational coordinate formulas, so the loci can be computed efficiently and plotted smoothly.
$$t = \tan\tfrac{\theta}{2}, \quad C = (\cos\theta,\, \sin\theta)$$
That substitution turns trigonometric motion into rational motion, which is especially useful for symbolic computation.
Configuration Details
The Thales Setup

Fixing $A=(1,0)$ and $B=(-1,0)$ with $C$ constrained to the unit circle means the circumradius is always $R=1$ and the circumcenter is always the origin $O=(0,0)$. This dramatically simplifies the barycentric weight expressions: quantities like $\cos A$, $\cos B$, and $\cos C$ reduce to rational functions of $t = \tan(\theta/2)$, enabling closed-form locus derivations via SymPy.

Locus Types Observed

Depending on the center, the traced locus may be a proper conic (ellipse, hyperbola, parabola), a circle, a line, or a higher-degree algebraic curve. The pipeline classifies each output using the compiled etc_centers_final.json and records the locus type in the barycentric search table.
